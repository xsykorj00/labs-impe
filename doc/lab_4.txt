4.  Sampling a continuous signal using an AD converter, processing of samples.

    • Assignment details; when solving this task, take a look on the Chapter 23
      Analog-to-Digital Converter (ADC) and the Laboratory Kit Schematic (e.g. to study
      the connection between the display and the MCU, and to identify the AD converter
      pins (channels) used for sampling a continuous quantity from the MCU interface):

        o After programming and running the embedded application will enter the infinite
          loop in main(), where it will continuously update the contents of the 4-digit
          7-segment LED display on the laboratory kit to display current voltage.

        o Complete the template embedded application, i.e. the bodies of the functions
          ADC0_Init() and ADC0_IRQHandler(), so that the resulting application behaves
          according to the following expected behaviour (see also comments in C template):

            - The AD converter (ADC) will sample the given continuous quantity
              continuously, with a conversion cycle determined by Bus clock/2 and
              clock divider = 8; the resulting ADC sample will be 8-bit, produced
              from a hardware-averaged series of 32 consecutive samples; the
              completion of each ADC sample will be signalled by an interrupt.

            - In the interrupt service routine, the resulting ADC sample will be
              read/stored, e.g. into a suitable local variable, and from its value
              it will be determined how many units, tenths, and hundredths of the
              measured quantity this sample represents. The obtained information
              will be correctly inserted into the "result" string for the purpose
              of displaying it on the laboratory kit display. Your task is to 
              complete these functions:
              + void ADC0_IRQHandler(void)
              + static void adc0_init(void)
              + int main(void)
